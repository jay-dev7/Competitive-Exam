🔵 What is a Tangent?
  • A tangent touches a circle at exactly one point (point of tangency)
  • Tangent is always perpendicular to the radius drawn to the point of contact
  • From an external point, exactly two tangents can be drawn
  • Both tangents from same external point are equal in length
  • A tangent never passes through the interior of the circle
📋 Types & Key Theorem
🏛 Tangent from External Point Theorem
The lengths of two tangents drawn from an external point to a circle are equal.
AB = AC (both from external point A)
⚠️ Important Properties
OB ⊥ AB  and  OC ⊥ AC
∠OAB = ∠OAC (OA bisects angle between tangents)
OA bisects ∠BOC
📏 Length of Tangent Formula
🔑 Core Formula
In right triangle OTP:
OP² = OT² + PT²
d² = r² + l²
∴ l = √(d² − r²)

where: l = tangent length, r = radius, d = distance from center to external point
▶ Example 1 — Basic Tangent Length
Q: A point P is 13 cm from center O of a circle of radius 5 cm. Find the length of tangent from P.
d = 13 cm, r = 5 cm
l = √(d² − r²)
l = √(169 − 25)
l = √144
∴ l = 12 cm ✅
▶ Example 2 — Find Radius
Q: Tangent length = 8 cm, distance = 10 cm. Find radius.
l = 8, d = 10
r = √(d² − l²) = √(100 − 64) = √36
∴ r = 6 cm ✅
▶ Example 3 — Find Distance
Q: Tangent = 15 cm, radius = 9 cm. Find distance of external point from center.
d = √(l² + r²) = √(225 + 81) = √306
d = √306 = 3√34 ≈ 17.49 cm
∴ d = 3√34 ≈ 17.5 cm ✅
✌️ Two Tangents from External Point
PA = PBBoth tangent lengths equal
PA = PB= √(OP² − r²)
∠OAP = ∠OBP= 90°
OP bisects ∠APB∠APO = ∠BPO
OP bisects ∠AOB∠AOP = ∠BOP
OAPB is a kiteKite shape!
▶ PA = PB = 6, r = 4.5. Find OP.
OP = √(PA² + r²) = √(36 + 20.25)
OP = √56.25 = 7.5 cm
OP = 7.5 cm ✅
📐 Angle Between Two Tangents
🔑 Key Relationship
∠APB + ∠AOB = 180°
θ + α = 180°
So: ∠APB = 180° − ∠AOB
And: ∠APO = 90° − ∠AOP
▶ ∠AOB = 110°. Find ∠APB.
∠APB + ∠AOB = 180°
∠APB = 180° − 110° = 70°
∠APB = 70° ✅
🔷 Angles in Tangent–Radius Configuration
🏛 Tangent–Chord Angle (Alternate Segment Theorem)
Angle between a tangent and a chord = inscribed angle on the opposite side (alternate segment).
∠(tangent, chord) = ∠(inscribed angle in alternate segment)
▶ Tangent–chord angle = 65°. Find the inscribed angle in alternate segment.
By Alternate Segment Theorem:
Inscribed angle = Tangent–chord angle
∠ in alternate segment = 65° ✅
🧮 Important Angle Formulas Summary
Tangent ⊥ Radius∠OTP = 90°
∠APB + ∠AOB= 180°
∠OAP = ∠OBP= 90°
In OAPB∠A + ∠B + ∠O + ∠P = 360°
90+90+∠O+∠P= 360°
∠O + ∠P= 180°
sin(∠APO)= r/d
cos(∠APO)= l/d
tan(∠APO)= r/l
▶ Example: OP = 10, r = 6. Find ∠APO and tangent length.
l = √(100−36) = √64 = 8 cm
sin(∠APO) = r/d = 6/10 = 0.6 → ∠APO = 37° (approx)
∴ ∠APB = 2 × 37° = 74°
l = 8 cm, ∠APB ≈ 74° ✅
⭕ Common Tangents to Two Circles

Two circles can have 0 to 4 common tangents depending on their positions. This is one of the most asked topics in SSC CGL!

d > r₁ + r₂4 common tangents
d = r₁ + r₂3 tangents (ext. tangency)
|r₁−r₂| < d < r₁+r₂2 tangents
d = |r₁ − r₂|1 tangent (int. tangency)
d < |r₁ − r₂|0 tangents (one inside)
↔️ Length of External Common Tangent
🔑 External Tangent Length
L_ext = √(d² − (r₁−r₂)²)
▶ d=13, r₁=5, r₂=2. Find L_ext.
L = √(13² − (5−2)²) = √(169 − 9) = √160
L_ext = 4√10 ≈ 12.65 cm ✅
✕ Length of Internal Common Tangent
🔑 Internal Tangent Length
L_int = √(d² − (r₁+r₂)²)
Only valid when d > r₁ + r₂
▶ d=17, r₁=4, r₂=9. Find L_int.
L = √(17² − (4+9)²) = √(289 − 169) = √120
L_int = 2√30 ≈ 10.95 cm ✅

⚡ Power of a Point Theorem
🔑 Power of a Point
PT² = PA × PB
(tangent)² = (external secant) × (whole secant)

Also: if two chords intersect inside circle:
PA × PB = PC × PD
▶ PA=4, PB=9. Find tangent PT.
PT² = PA × PB = 4 × 9 = 36
PT = 6 cm ✅
🔀 Tangent–Secant Relationship
🏛 Tangent–Secant Theorem
AT² = AB × AC
where AB = external segment, AC = whole secant length
▶ AT=8, AB=4. Find BC (chord).
AT² = AB × AC
64 = 4 × AC → AC = 16
BC = AC − AB = 16 − 4 = 12 cm
BC = 12 cm ✅
✏️ Practice Questions
01
EASY
A tangent is drawn from a point P to a circle of radius 5 cm. If OP = 13 cm, find the length of the tangent.
r = 5, OP = 13
l = √(OP² − r²) = √(169 − 25) = √144
l = 12 cm ✅
02
EASY
Two tangents PA and PB are drawn from an external point P to a circle with centre O and radius 7 cm. If ∠APB = 60°, find OP.
∠APB = 60° → ∠APO = 30° (OA bisects ∠APB)
sin(∠APO) = r/OP → sin 30° = 7/OP
0.5 = 7/OP → OP = 14 cm
OP = 14 cm ✅
03
MEDIUM
From an external point P, tangent PA = 10 cm is drawn to a circle. A secant PAB is drawn with PA = 5 cm (external part). Find AB (chord length).
PT² = Pext × Pwhole (Tangent-Secant)
10² = 5 × PAB_whole
PAB_whole = 100/5 = 20
AB = 20 − 5 = 15 cm
AB = 15 cm ✅
04
MEDIUM
Two circles have radii 8 cm and 3 cm. The distance between their centres is 13 cm. Find the length of the external common tangent.
L_ext = √(d² − (r₁−r₂)²)
= √(169 − (8−3)²) = √(169 − 25)
= √144
L_ext = 12 cm ✅
05
MEDIUM
Two circles have radii 5 cm and 3 cm, centres 10 cm apart. Find the length of the internal common tangent.
L_int = √(d² − (r₁+r₂)²)
= √(100 − (5+3)²) = √(100 − 64)
= √36
L_int = 6 cm ✅
06
HARD
PA and PB are tangents from P to a circle with centre O. If PA = 24 cm and OP = 26 cm, find the area of quadrilateral OAPB.
r = OA = √(OP²−PA²) = √(676−576) = √100 = 10
OAPB is a kite (OA=OB=r, PA=PB=24)
Area = ½ × d₁ × d₂ (diagonals of kite)
d₁ = OP = 26, d₂ = 2×PA×OA/OP = 2×24×10/26
Actually: Area = 2 × area(△OAP) = 2 × ½ × OA × PA
= OA × PA = 10 × 24 = 240
Area = 240 cm² ✅
07
HARD
Tangent from point P touches the circle at T. A chord AB passes through the centre. If PT = 12 and PB = 8 cm, find PA.
By Power of a Point: PT² = PA × PB
144 = PA × 8
PA = 144/8 = 18 cm
PA = 18 cm ✅
08
HARD
Two tangents from P make an angle of 120° with each other. The radius of the circle is 3√3 cm. Find the distance OP.
∠APB = 120° → ∠APO = 60°
sin(60°) = r/OP = OA/OP
√3/2 = 3√3 / OP
OP = 3√3 × 2/√3 = 6 cm
OP = 6 cm ✅
⚡ Exam Tricks & Quick Reference
⚡ Trick 1 — Golden Formula

For ANY tangent problem: identify r, d (or l), and use l² + r² = d². This single right triangle relationship solves 80% of tangent questions!

l = √(d²−r²) · r = √(d²−l²) · d = √(l²+r²)
⚡ Trick 2 — Angle shortcut

When tangent angle ∠APB is given:
• sin(∠APO) = r/OP
• ∠APO = ½ × ∠APB
Use 30°→sin=½, 60°→sin=√3/2 for clean answers!

⚡ Trick 3 — Power of a Point

Whenever you see tangent + secant from same point: PT² = PA × PB. Look for Pythagorean triples hidden here. PA=4, PB=9 → PT=6 (instant!).

⚡ Trick 4 — Two circles tangent key

External tangent formula uses (r₁ − r₂) — difference of radii.
Internal tangent formula uses (r₁ + r₂) — sum of radii.
Easy to remember: I→Internal→Add, E→External→rEduction!

⚡ Trick 5 — Kite property

OAPB (two tangents + two radii) is always a kite. Area = OA × PA = r × l. Saves time — no need to compute full quadrilateral!

⚡ Trick 6 — Number of common tangents

Memorise with: "4-3-2-1-0" as circles move closer:
Separated → 4, External touch → 3, Intersecting → 2, Internal touch → 1, One inside → 0

⚡ Trick 7 — Pythagorean triple hunter

In exam: if r=5 and d=13 → l=12 (5-12-13 triple). If r=3, d=5 → l=4 (3-4-5). Always scan for Pythagorean triples first — saves 30 seconds!

⚡ Trick 8 — Equal tangent usage

If a triangle is inscribed/circumscribed in a circle, use equal tangent lengths from each vertex to find side lengths. Let tangents from A=x, B=y, C=z, then sides: a=y+z, b=x+z, c=x+y.
