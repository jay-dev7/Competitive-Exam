⚡ Exam Tricks & Shortcuts
⚡ Trick 1 — Cylinder vs Cone

Volume of cone = ⅓ × Volume of cylinder (same r & h). So if you melt a cylinder into a cone of same base, height of cone = 3 × height of cylinder.

⚡ Trick 2 — Sphere melted into cones

If a sphere of radius R is melted into n cones of radius r and height h:
n = (4/3 πR³) ÷ (⅓ πr²h) = 4R³ / r²h

⚡ Trick 3 — Cube diagonal shortcut

If space diagonal of cube is given = d, then
side a = d / √3 and Volume = (d/√3)³

⚡ Trick 4 — TSA vs CSA check

For cylinder: TSA = CSA + 2πr² (add two circles). For cone: TSA = CSA + πr² (add one base circle). Easy to remember!

⚡ Trick 5 — Hemisphere vs Sphere

Hemisphere Volume = ½ × sphere volume.
Hemisphere CSA = ½ × sphere SA = 2πr².
But TSA = 3πr² (add the flat circular base).

⚡ Trick 6 — Wire/sheet problems

When a shape is recast: Volume stays the same. Set V₁ = V₂ and solve for unknown dimension. Works for cylinder→sphere, cone→cylinder etc.

⚡ Trick 7 — Water level problems

Volume of object submerged = Volume of water displaced. For cylinder container: rise in level = V(object) / πR² where R = radius of container.

⚡ Trick 8 — Slant height shortcut

For cone: if r and h form a Pythagorean triple (e.g. 6-8-10), slant height l = hypotenuse directly. No calculation needed!
