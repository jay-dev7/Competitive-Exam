⚡ Trick 1 — a²+b² shortcut

Given a+b and ab: a²+b² = (a+b)²−2ab. No need to find a and b. Given a−b and ab: a²+b² = (a−b)²+2ab.

⚡ Trick 2 — x+1/x chain

k=x+1/x → x²+1/x²=k²−2 → x³+1/x³=k³−3k → x⁴+1/x⁴=(k²−2)²−2. Build the chain step by step!

⚡ Trick 3 — a+b+c=0 magic

Then a³+b³+c³=3abc always. Also (a²+b²+c²)/(ab+bc+ca)=−2. Spot the "sum=0" condition quickly!

⚡ Trick 4 — Surd memorisation

Memorise: √8=2√2, √12=2√3, √18=3√2, √27=3√3, √48=4√3, √50=5√2, √72=6√2, √75=5√3. Saves 30 seconds per question!

⚡ Trick 5 — Rationalise instantly

Denominator (a+√b): multiply by (a−√b). Result denominator = a²−b. For (√a+√b): denominator becomes a−b.

⚡ Trick 6 — (a+b)²−(a−b)²=4ab

If given (a+b)²=X and (a−b)²=Y: then 4ab=X−Y, and a²+b²=(X+Y)/2. Very fast for competitive exams!
