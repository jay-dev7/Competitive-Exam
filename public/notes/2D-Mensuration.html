⚡ Exam Tricks & Shortcuts
⚡ Trick 1 — Square from diagonal

If diagonal of square = d, then Area = d²/2. No need to find side first! Example: diagonal=10 → Area = 100/2 = 50 cm².

⚡ Trick 2 — Rectangle diagonal shortcut

If l:b = 3:4 and diagonal given, use 3-4-5 triple. Diagonal=10 → l=6, b=8. Area = 6×8 = 48 instantly!

⚡ Trick 3 — Circle from circumference

If Circumference = C, then Area = C²/(4π). Example: C=44 → Area = 1936/(4×22/7) = 1936×7/88 = 154 cm².

⚡ Trick 4 — Rhombus side

Diagonals of rhombus bisect at 90°. Each side² = (d₁/2)² + (d₂/2)². Use Pythagorean triples like 3-4-5, 5-12-13!

⚡ Trick 5 — Area change on scaling

If all dimensions are multiplied by k, Area becomes k² × original. Perimeter becomes k × original. Volume (3D) becomes k³ × original.

⚡ Trick 6 — Equilateral triangle shortcut

Area of equilateral triangle with side a = √3/4 × a². For a=2: Area=√3. For a=4: Area=4√3. Multiply by 4 each time sides double.

⚡ Trick 7 — Path around a field

For a rectangular field l×b with a path of width w inside: Inner rectangle = (l−2w)×(b−2w). Area of path = Area(outer) − Area(inner).

⚡ Trick 8 — Heron's formula quick check

If sides form a right triangle (Pythagorean triple), use Area = ½×leg₁×leg₂ directly. Heron's formula is only needed for general triangles!
