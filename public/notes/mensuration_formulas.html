🔷 2D Shapes — Area & Perimeter

Triangle

General
Area½ × base × height
Perimetera + b + c
Heron's√[s(s−a)(s−b)(s−c)]
Semi-perims = (a+b+c)/2
📌 Scenario Variants
Right △: Area = ½ × leg₁ × leg₂
Equilateral: Area = (√3/4) × a²
Isosceles: Area = (b/4)√(4a²−b²)
Given 2 sides & angle: ½ab·sinC

Right Triangle

90° at C

Perimetera + b + c
Inradius r(a + b − c) / 2

Rhombus

Equal sides, diagonals ⊥

Trapezium

One pair of parallel sides
Area½(a+b) × h
Perimetera + b + c + d
Median (m)(a+b)/2

Quadrilateral

General / Irregular
Area½ × d × (h₁+h₂)
Noted = diagonal
h₁,h₂ = perp. heights

Ring (Annulus)

R = outer, r = inner
Areaπ(R²−r²)
Perimeter2π(R+r)
📌 Variants
Area = π(R+r)(R−r)
Width = R − r
⬡ Regular Polygons — n-sided Shapes
⚙️ Universal Formulas — Any Regular n-gon (side = a)
Area = (n·a²/4) × cot(π/n)
Perimeter = n × a
Interior angle = (n−2) × 180 / n
Exterior angle = 360 / n
Sum of interior ∠ = (n−2) × 180°
Inradius r = (a/2) × cot(π/n)
Circumradius R = (a/2) × cosec(π/n)
No. of diagonals = n(n−3)/2
💡 Also: Area = ½ × Perimeter × inradius = ½ × n × a × r

🎯 Exam Tricks & Shortcuts
🔑 Must-Know Relations
Cone Vol = ⅓ × Cylinder Vol (same r, h)
Sphere SA = 4 × Circle Area (same r)
Hemisphere Vol = ½ Sphere Vol
Equilateral: R = 2r (circum = 2 × inradius)
Square Area = ½ × diagonal²
⚡ Perimeter → Area Shortcuts
Circle: C given → r = C/2π → A = πr²
Square: P given → a = P/4 → A = a²
Equilateral: P given → a = P/3 → A = √3a²/4
General △: Area = r × s (inradius × semi-perimeter)
📏 Scaling Rules
Scale sides by k → Area scales by k²
Scale sides by k → Volume scales by k³
Scale sides by k → Perimeter scales by k
Radius doubled → Vol × 8, SA × 4
🔢 Pythagorean Triples (Right Triangle r)
3-4-5 → r = 1 | R = 2.5
6-8-10 → r = 2 | R = 5
5-12-13 → r = 2 | R = 6.5
8-15-17 → r = 3 | R = 8.5
7-24-25 → r = 3 | R = 12.5
🎯 Decision Tree — Which Formula?
3 sides given? → Heron's formula
Right △ (legs given)? → ½ab, hyp=√(a²+b²)
Equilateral? → Area=√3a²/4
P + r given? → Area = r × P/2
Area + P given? → r = 2·Area/P
💡 Melting / Recasting Trick
Volume is conserved when shape is recast

n small spheres from big sphere:
n = R³ / r³

Wire from cylinder (radius ρ, length L):
πR²H = πρ²L → L = R²H/ρ²
