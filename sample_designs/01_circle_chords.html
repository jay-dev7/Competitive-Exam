📋 Key Properties
5 Essential Facts
1. Diameter is the longest chord
2. Equal chords are equidistant from centre
3. Perpendicular from centre bisects chord
4. Tangent ⊥ radius at point of contact
5. Angle in semicircle = 90°
Chord ↔ Distance Formula
If chord length = 2l, distance from centre = d, radius = r:
r² = l² + d²
Chord = 2√(r² − d²)
▶ r=13, chord=10. Find distance from centre.
Half chord l = 5
d = √(r²−l²) = √(169−25) = √144
d = 12 cm ✓

📐 Equal Chords
Equal Chord Theorems
• Equal chords → equal distance from centre
• Equidistant chords → equal length
• Chord closer to centre → longer
• Diameter = longest chord (d = 0)
▶ AB = CD = 24cm, r=13. Find distance from O.
l = 12, d = √(169 − 144) = √25
d = 5 cm (same for both) ✓
📊 Two Parallel Chords
Distance Between Parallel Chords
Same side of centre: dist = |d₁ − d₂|
Opposite sides: dist = d₁ + d₂
▶ r=10, chords 12cm & 16cm (same side). Distance between?
d₁=√(100−36)=8, d₂=√(100−64)=6
Distance = 8 − 6 = 2 cm ✓
🔗 Intersecting Chords (Inside)
Intersecting Chords Theorem
PA × PB = PC × PD
▶ PA=3, PB=12, PC=4. Find PD.
3 × 12 = 4 × PD → PD = 36/4
PD = 9 cm ✓
▶ PA=6, PB=8. Chord CD is bisected at P, find CD.
PA×PB = PC×PD. PC=PD (bisected)
48 = PC² → PC = 4√3
CD = 8√3 cm ✓
🔀 Secant-Secant (External)
External Point Theorem
PA × PB = PC × PD
(external × whole = external × whole)
▶ PA=4, AB=8, PC=3. Find CD.
PB=PA+AB=12. PA×PB=PC×PD → 4×12=3×PD
PD=16. CD=PD−PC=16−3=13
CD = 13 cm ✓

Chords intersect inside∠ = ½(arc₁+arc₂)

Tangent-chord= inscribed angle (alt.seg)
Tangent-tangent from P∠P + ∠O = 180°

🔷 Cyclic Quadrilateral
Cyclic Quad Laws
∠A + ∠C = 180° (opposite angles)
∠B + ∠D = 180°
Exterior angle = opposite interior angle
Ptolemy: AC·BD = AB·CD + AD·BC

✅ Is it Cyclic?
Quick Check
If opposite angles sum to 180° → cyclic
If not → NOT inscribable in a circle
Rectangle: always cyclic (all 90° angles)
Parallelogram: cyclic only if it's a rectangle

⚡ Formula 1 — Chord & Distance

Always: r² = (half-chord)² + d². Chord = 2√(r²−d²). Just Pythagoras in a circle. Works in every chord problem!

⚡ Formula 2 — Intersecting Chords

Inside: PA×PB = PC×PD. Outside (secants): same formula! Tangent: PT² = PA×PB. All three are "power of a point".

⚡ Formula 3 — Central vs Inscribed

Central angle = 2 × inscribed angle on same arc. Angle in semicircle = 90° (inscribed angle on diameter). Exam favourite!

⚡ Formula 4 — Angles at intersection

Chords inside: angle = ½(sum of arcs). Secants outside: angle = ½(difference of arcs). Remember: inside=sum, outside=diff.

⚡ Formula 5 — Cyclic Quad

Opposite angles add to 180°. Test: A+C=180 AND B+D=180. If only one holds, check calculation. Both must hold simultaneously.

⚡ Formula 6 — Equal Chords

Equal chords → equal distances from centre. If d₁=d₂ → chord₁=chord₂. Closer to centre = longer chord. Diameter = maximum chord.
