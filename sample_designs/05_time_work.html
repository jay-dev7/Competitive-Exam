📐 Core Concept
Golden Triangle
Work = Time × Efficiency
If a person does 1 job in N days:
→ Daily work = 1/N (fraction of total)
→ N days work = 1 complete job
A does job in N daysA's 1-day work = 1/N
A+B together1-day work = 1/A + 1/B
Time for A+B togetherT = AB/(A+B)
Work = 1 (whole job)always treat as 1
▶ A: 12 days, B: 8 days. Together?
Together = 1/12 + 1/8 = 2/24 + 3/24 = 5/24
Time = 24/5
= 4.8 days ✓
🔢 LCM Method (Fastest!)
Assign Total Work = LCM of all days
1. Find LCM of all time values
2. Each person's efficiency = LCM ÷ their days
3. Combined efficiency = sum of all efficiencies
4. Time together = Total work ÷ combined eff.
▶ A: 12 days, B: 8 days. Use LCM method.
LCM(12,8) = 24 (total work units)
A's efficiency = 24/12 = 2 units/day
B's efficiency = 24/8 = 3 units/day
Together = 5 units/day
Time = 24/5 = 4.8 days ✓
▶ A: 10 days, B: 15 days, C: 20 days. Together?
LCM=60. A=6, B=4, C=3 units/day
Together=13 units/day
Time=60/13≈4.6 days ✓
👥 Two-Person Problems
A alone in a, B alone in bTogether: ab/(a+b) days
A+B together in T, A alone in aB = aT/(a−T)
A faster than B by k timesB takes k×A's time
▶ A+B together: 6 days. A alone: 9 days. B alone?
1/B = 1/6 − 1/9 = 3/18 − 2/18 = 1/18
B = 18 days ✓
▶ A does in 20 days. After 4 days A leaves, B finishes rest in 24 days. B alone?
A does 4/20=1/5 in 4 days. Remaining=4/5
B does 4/5 in 24 days → B does 1 job in 24×5/4=30 days
B = 30 days ✓
👥👥 Three-Person & Alternating Work
▶ A,B,C together: 4 days. A+B: 6, B+C: 8. Find A+C.
A+B+C=1/4. A+B=1/6, B+C=1/8
C=1/4−1/6=1/12. A=1/4−1/8=1/8
A+C=1/8+1/12=3/24+2/24=5/24

LCM=20. A=2/day, B=1/day. Per 2 days=3 units
After 12 days: 18 units done. Remaining=2

13 days ✓
🚰 Pipes & Cisterns Logic
Key Rule
Inlet pipe fills (+)
Outlet/leak empties (−)
Net rate = (filling rate) − (emptying rate)
Pipe A fills in a hoursRate = +1/a
Pipe B empties in b hoursRate = −1/b
Both open: time to fill= ab/(b−a) (if b>a)
▶ Inlet fills in 6h, outlet empties in 12h. Both open. Fill time?
Net rate = 1/6 − 1/12 = 2/12 − 1/12 = 1/12
12 hours to fill ✓
▶ 3 pipes A,B,C. A fills in 4h, B in 6h, C empties in 12h. All open: time?
Net rate = 1/4+1/6−1/12=3/12+2/12−1/12=4/12=1/3
3 hours to fill ✓
🔧 Leak Problems
Without leak, fills in a hoursNormal rate = 1/a
With leak, fills in b hoursActual rate = 1/b
Leak rate= 1/a − 1/b
Leak empties full tank inab/(b−a) hours
▶ Pipe fills in 20h. With leak: fills in 30h. Leak empties in?
Leak rate = 1/20 − 1/30 = 3/60 − 2/60 = 1/60
Leak empties full tank in 60 hours ✓
▶ Tank is full. Inlet: 5h, outlet: 3h. Both open. Empty in?
Net emptying rate = 1/3 − 1/5 = 5/15 − 3/15 = 2/15
Tank empties in 15/2 = 7.5 hours ✓
💰 Wages Distribution
Wages Principle
Wages are distributed in proportion to work done.
If A does 2/5 of work → A gets 2/5 of wages.
A:B efficiency ratio= their wage ratio
A takes a days, B takes b daysWages: B:A = a:b? No! 1/a:1/b = b:a
▶ A: 20 days, B: 30 days. Total wage ₹1500. A's share?
Efficiency: A=1/20, B=1/30
Ratio: 1/20 : 1/30 = 3:2. Total=5 parts
A's share = (3/5)×1500
A gets ₹900 ✓
▶ A+B+C finish in 10 days, A alone 20 days, B alone 30 days. Total: ₹6000. C's share?
LCM=60. A=3, B=2. A+B+C=6. C=1/day
C's ratio = 1/6 of work
C's wage = 6000×1/6 = ₹1000 ✓

📊 Men-Days-Work Formula
M×D×H = W (core formula)
M×D = W (if equal daily hours)
M₁D₁/W₁ = M₂D₂/W₂
Double men→ Half the time
Triple work, same men→ Triple the time
Efficiency↑2x→ Time↓ 2x
▶ 8 men do a job in 12 days. 6 men for same job?
M₁D₁=M₂D₂ → 8×12=6×D₂
D₂ = 16 days ✓

⚡ Trick 1 — LCM is King

Always use LCM of all time values as total work. Then efficiency = LCM÷days. Add efficiencies. Time = Total÷combined efficiency. No fractions, no confusion!

⚡ Trick 2 — Two-person shortcut

A in 'a' days, B in 'b' days. Together = ab/(a+b). Memorise this formula. Also: if together=T and one alone=A: B = AT/(A−T).

⚡ Trick 3 — Wages proportional to work

Work ratio = efficiency ratio = 1/days ratio. So if A takes a days, B takes b days: A's wage : B's wage = b : a (days are inverted!).

⚡ Trick 4 — Pipes same as work

Filling pipe = positive worker. Outlet/leak = negative worker. Net rate = sum of all (with signs). Time = 1/net rate.

⚡ Trick 5 — Men-Days inverse rule

More men → fewer days. M₁D₁ = M₂D₂ (same work). To find extra men: compute M₂−M₁. Include efficiency if workers have different rates.

⚡ Trick 6 — A+B+C from pairs

Given A+B, B+C, A+C: 2(A+B+C) = sum of all three pairs. Find A+B+C first, then individual from the pairs.
